The Issue

There is a wealth of human talent and cultural intelligence that remain untapped and undervalued in Australia. Senior leadership roles in major organisations and institutions do not genuinely reflect Australia's multiculturalness. We must tackle this.

We accommodate, co-operate and compromise ourselves often through our own silence and therefore validate the stereotypes held of us.

DAWN’s two-part workshop will explore the implications of this psycho-cultural unknowing to our careers and help you reach your leadership goals by developing strategies to overcome barriers like the Bamboo Ceiling.

DAWN is a professional and social network that promotes an inclusive Australia with culturally diverse leadership. Our mission is to unlock the talent from within Australia’s culturally diverse workforce to help business and government succeed.

Workshop 1

THE BAMBOO CURTAIN:
Myth or me?

Understand what factors shape your psycho-cultural identity Explore who we are as a socio-cultural being – Who am I? Which bits of me are Asian and Australian? The ‘Hands’ and ‘Chopsticks’ categories

Increase intercultural awareness from a position of unconscious to conscious knowingness

Develop tools to overcome barriers to leadership roles: The bamboo ceiling Focus on the universal values and appreciate the specific differences in managing cross-cultural encounters

Workshop 2

LIFTING THE CURTAIN:
Managing across cultures for global leadership

Increase your understanding of your own unconscious cultures.

Have greater awareness of how your unconscious cultural complex/syndrome shapes their managerial style

Be able to develop a comparative perspective of your own culture and western cultures

Develop tools to further self-understanding and raise the bamboo curtain for furthering your careers
